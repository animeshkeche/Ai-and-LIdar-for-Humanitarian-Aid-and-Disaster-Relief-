🔹 1. Flood Prediction & Early Warning System
🎯 Objective:

Predict flood-prone areas and issue real-time alerts.

⚙️ Core Components:

Collect live weather, rainfall, and river-level data via APIs (OpenWeatherMap, NASA).

Preprocess and visualize historical flood data.

Train an ML model (LSTM/Random Forest) for rainfall and flood prediction.

Deploy web dashboard with interactive maps (e.g., Folium or Google Maps API).

Send alerts via SMS or email when thresholds are exceeded.

🧰 Tools:

Python, TensorFlow, GeoPandas, Streamlit, Folium, OpenWeather API.

💡 Ideal For:

Students/researchers demonstrating AI + real-time integration.

🔹 2. Damage Assessment Dashboard
🎯 Objective:

Automatically detect and classify post-disaster damage using image analysis.

⚙️ Core Components:

Input: Satellite or drone imagery (before and after disaster).

Model: CNN/UNet to classify “undamaged”, “partially damaged”, “destroyed”.

Output: Color-coded map of affected regions.

Add filters for location, damage type, or severity.

🧰 Tools:

PyTorch, OpenCV, Detectron2, Google Earth Engine, Streamlit Dashboard.

💡 Ideal For:

Research on computer vision in humanitarian AI; scalable to multiple disasters (floods, fires, quakes).

🔹 3. Resource Allocation Optimizer
🎯 Objective:

Plan and optimize relief logistics (routes, supply chain, personnel deployment).

⚙️ Core Components:

Input: Demand and supply data (relief centers, stock, roads).

Model: Reinforcement Learning or Genetic Algorithm.

Output: Optimal routes and resource allocation plans.

Visualization: Map interface showing delivery paths.

🧰 Tools:

Python (SimPy, PuLP, or OR-Tools), Flask, Mapbox API, Streamlit.

💡 Ideal For:

AI + Operations Research / optimization projects.

🔹 4. Multi-purpose Disaster Management Platform
🎯 Objective:

Integrate prediction, monitoring, and response into one unified system.

⚙️ Core Components:

Prediction module: AI model for flood or fire forecasting.

Damage analysis module: Image-based detection.

Relief coordination module: Resource planning and dashboard.

Communication module: Chatbot for victim registration and info.

🧰 Tools:

TensorFlow, FastAPI backend, React/Streamlit frontend, PostgreSQL, AWS.

💡 Ideal For:

Capstone projects or production-ready systems combining multiple AI capabilities.

🔹 5. Educational / Demonstration Platform
🎯 Objective:

Showcase AI techniques used in disaster management using sample datasets.

⚙️ Core Components:

Multiple tabs for:

Disaster prediction (ML)

Damage classification (CV)

Relief optimization (RL)

Preloaded datasets for learning and experimentation.

Explanations and model visualizations.

🧰 Tools:

Streamlit or Gradio, sample data (e.g., Kaggle disaster datasets).

💡 Ideal For:

Teaching, awareness campaigns, or academic demonstration.

🔹 6. Optional: Chatbot for Disaster Relief
🎯 Objective:

An NLP chatbot to provide real-time assistance during disasters.

Communicates in multiple languages.

Provides evacuation info, relief center locations, and SOS registration.

🧰 Tools:
